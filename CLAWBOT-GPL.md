## Preamble

The Clawbot GPL is a free software license that builds upon the GNU General Public License Version 3 (GPL v3), extending it with the principles of Clawbot: **limitless openness**, **adaptability**, **resilience**, and **global inclusivity**. It incorporates The Burgess Principle — that a license itself must be open, extensible, and adaptable for future needs.

Traditional copyleft licenses protect freedom by ensuring that derivative works remain free. The Clawbot GPL goes further: it actively encourages universal collaboration, self-improving ecosystems, collective governance, accessibility-first design, and ethical AI integration, while preserving every guarantee of GPL v3.

All terms of the [GNU General Public License Version 3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.html) apply to works covered by this license. The additional clauses below supplement — and never restrict — those terms.

## Additional Terms and Clauses

### 1. Limitless Openness

**1.1 Universal License Compatibility**
Works licensed under the Clawbot GPL may be freely merged, combined, or integrated with any other open-source licensed work, regardless of the specific open-source license governing that other work (including, without limitation, MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD, MPL, LGPL, and Creative Commons licenses), provided that:

**1.2 No Proprietary Lock-In**
No contributor or distributor may impose technical or legal measures that restrict another person's ability to use, study, modify, share, or integrate the work with other open systems. Digital rights management (DRM) and similar encumbrance mechanisms are prohibited for any component covered under this license.

**1.3 Open Interfaces**
Public APIs, data formats, and communication protocols exposed by a covered work must be documented and made available under terms no more restrictive than this license, ensuring seamless interoperability with other open systems.

### 2. Adaptability and Self-Improvement

**2.1 Forks and Contributions**
Every fork of a work under the Clawbot GPL is explicitly encouraged. Forks must:

  (a) Be published in a publicly accessible location (e.g., a public version-control repository) within 90 days of their first public use or distribution; and  
  (b) Carry a clear notice identifying the upstream project from which the fork originated, including a link to the upstream source.

**2.2 Automated Dependency Management**
Distributors of covered works are encouraged to maintain automated tooling (e.g., Dependabot, Renovate, or equivalent) to keep dependencies up to date and to address known security vulnerabilities in a timely manner. Where such tooling is used, its configuration must be included in the distributed source code.

**2.3 Accessibility Enhancements as Contributions**
Any contributor who improves the accessibility of a covered work (see also Clause 4) is considered to have made a contribution under this license. Such contributions must be accepted for review by the project's maintainers within a reasonable time.

**2.4 Self-Healing and Resilience Mechanisms**
Covered works are encouraged to implement self-healing mechanisms — automated health checks, fallback behaviors, and error recovery routines — that reduce single points of failure. Where such mechanisms exist, their source code must be included in the distributed source.

### 3. Resilience Against Sabotage

**3.1 Collective Ownership**
No single individual, organization, or legal entity may hold exclusive control over the canonical distribution channel, governance process, or primary repository of a covered work. Projects are encouraged to adopt distributed governance models (e.g., multi-maintainer stewardship, foundation governance, or community voting mechanisms).

**3.2 Anti-Capture Provisions**
Any attempt by a single entity to:

  (a) Revoke, relicense, or re-close any version of the covered work that has already been distributed under this license;  
  (b) Remove, alter, or obscure attribution notices required by this license; or  
  (c) Use patents, trademarks, or other intellectual property mechanisms to prevent recipients from exercising the rights granted herein,

shall be considered a material breach of this license. Upon such breach, that entity's rights under this license terminate automatically, while the rights of all other recipients remain unaffected.

**3.3 Distributed Source Hosting**
Maintainers are encouraged to mirror the source code of covered works across multiple independent hosting services to ensure continuity of access and to prevent loss of availability due to the failure or censorship of any single platform.

**3.4 Succession and Continuity**
Projects using this license are encouraged to publish and maintain a succession plan that names alternative maintainers or governance bodies who may assume stewardship in the event that existing maintainers become unavailable.

### 4. Global Inclusivity

**4.1 Accessibility-First Requirements**
All user-facing interfaces distributed as part of a covered work must make reasonable efforts to comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA or a successor standard. Where full compliance is not immediately achievable, the project must maintain a public accessibility roadmap describing planned improvements and target timelines.

**4.2 Multi-Language Support**
User-facing text in covered works should be structured to support internationalization (i18n) and localization (l10n). Where string externalization frameworks or translation infrastructure already exist in a project, new user-facing strings must be added in a manner compatible with that infrastructure.

**4.3 Braille and Text-to-Speech Integration**
Where technically feasible, covered works that produce or display text content are encouraged to provide or document integration with Braille display standards and text-to-speech (TTS) engines, enabling access for users with visual impairments.

**4.4 No Geographic Discrimination**
The rights granted by this license apply equally to all users regardless of their country of residence, nationality, or jurisdiction. Distributors may not impose additional geographic restrictions beyond those required by applicable export control law.

### 5. AI and Automation Integration

**5.1 Open AI Integration**
Works licensed under the Clawbot GPL may be freely integrated with AI systems (including, without limitation, large language models, translation services such as DeepL, and machine learning platforms such as Hugging Face), provided that:

  (a) Any AI-generated output incorporated into a covered work is clearly attributed as AI-assisted; and  
  (b) The integration does not introduce proprietary lock-in or restrict users from replacing the AI component with a freely licensed alternative.

**5.2 Training Data Transparency**
Where a covered work itself trains or fine-tunes an AI model, the training dataset (or a clear description of its composition and provenance) must be made available to recipients alongside the model weights, in accordance with the source-availability requirements of GPL v3.

**5.3 Ethical AI Use**
Contributors and distributors of covered works must not use AI integrations for purposes that would violate any recipient's rights under this license, including automated surveillance, discriminatory profiling, or actions that undermine collective governance as described in Clause 3.

### 6. The Burgess Principle

**6.1 License Extensibility**
This license is itself open and extensible. Any person or organization may publish a revised version of the Clawbot GPL, provided that:

  (a) The revised version carries a new name or version number clearly distinguishing it from this version;  
  (b) The revised version does not remove or weaken any of the protections afforded by GPL v3; and  
  (c) The revised version is published under a license that is itself open and freely redistributable.

**6.2 Backward Compatibility**
Works licensed under any earlier version of the Clawbot GPL may, at the recipient's option, be used, modified, and distributed under the terms of any later version of the Clawbot GPL published by the original authors or their designated successors.

**6.3 Community Governance of this License**
Proposed amendments to this license should be discussed in a public forum with adequate notice (no less than 30 days) before adoption, allowing the community to review and comment.

## How the Clawbot GPL Improves Upon Traditional GPL

| Feature | GPL v3 | Clawbot GPL |
|---|---|---|
| Copyleft protection | ✅ | ✅ (inherited) |
| Patent and DRM protections | ✅ | ✅ (inherited) |
| License compatibility provisions | Limited | Universal open-source compatibility (§1.1) |
| Fork encouragement | Permitted | Actively encouraged with publication requirements (§2.1) |
| Automated security updates | Not addressed | Encouraged with source inclusion (§2.2) |
| Governance / anti-capture | Not addressed | Collective ownership and anti-capture clauses (§3.1–3.2) |
| Source continuity | Single source | Distributed hosting encouraged (§3.3) |
| Accessibility compliance | Not addressed | WCAG 2.1 AA required for user-facing works (§4.1) |
| Multi-language support | Not addressed | i18n/l10n requirements (§4.2) |
| Braille/TTS integration | Not addressed | Encouraged where feasible (§4.3) |
| AI integration | Not addressed | Explicitly permitted with transparency requirements (§5.1–5.3) |
| License extensibility | FSF controls updates | Community-governed, versioned extensions (§6.1–6.3) |
